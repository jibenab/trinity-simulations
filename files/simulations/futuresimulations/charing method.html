Friction does not create charge. It transfers electrons from one surface to the other through direct mechanical rubbing. The total charge of the pair stays zero.

Conduction transfers charge through direct physical contact. The excess electrons on the rod repel each other and flow into the neutral sphere until both reach the same potential.

Drag the rod close to the sphere to polarise it. Toggle the earth connection while the rod is near to permanently charge it.

Induction charges a conductor without contact. The nearby charge repels the sphere's free electrons. Earthing drains these repelled electrons away. When earthing is removed, the sphere is left with a positive charge.
