Key Concept: The Plumb Line Rule

An object is stable as long as the vertical line passing straight down through its Centre of Gravity falls within its base of support. Once the line crosses the edge of the base, the object will topple over.

Key Concept: Form Factor Matters

Objects with a lower Centre of Gravity and a wider base are inherently more stable. They require a much steeper angle or larger force to push their CoG vertical line outside their base.

Key Concept: Engineering for Stability

Real-world vehicles are designed with CoG in mind. Racing cars optimize for lowest possible CoG for speed in corners. Double-decker buses have strict regulations on top-heaviness (e.g., no standing on the upper deck) to prevent tipping on sloped roads.

Key Concept: Biomechanical Balance

The human body is highly dynamic. To maintain balance, your nervous system and muscles constantly shift your body parts to ensure your total Centre of Gravity remains over your base of support (your footprints). If it moves outside, you must step or fall.
