Water specifically, or a generic substance?

This affects whether transition temperatures are fixed or adjustable by the student.

A

Water — fixed at 0 °C and 100 °C

Familiar and curriculum-standard. Melting and boiling points are labelled and cannot be moved. Good for learning the shape of the heating curve.

B

Generic substance — adjustable transition temperatures

Students drag sliders to set melting point and boiling point, and watch the curve reshape. More exploratory; teaches the concept rather than one example.

C

Water by default, but unlockable

Starts as water so it matches textbook diagrams. An "Advanced" toggle reveals sliders to shift the transition temperatures and compare substances.
